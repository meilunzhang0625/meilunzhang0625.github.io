About Me

Welcome! I am a PhD student at the University of Washington’s School of Oceanography. My research uses geochemical proxies preserved in marine sediments to investigate past and present changes in the ocean carbon cycle and their role in climate, with a particular focus on the North Pacific. I apply a range of tracers, including stable and radiocarbon isotopes in seawater and foraminifera, to reconstruct ocean ventilation, carbon storage, marine productivity, and ocean hypoxia.

Outside of my research, I enjoy exploring new restaurants and trying different bubble teas. Feel free to reach out via the Contact page — I am always happy to discuss ideas or potential collaborations.
